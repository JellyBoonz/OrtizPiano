## **1. A Character (Your Customer)**

**Who is your ideal customer?**

Piano owners who genuinely care about the quality and condition of their instrument — musicians, parents of budding young players, and families who see their piano as more than furniture. These are people who value the craft of music and want their piano to reflect that care, whether it's a treasured heirloom or a daily practice instrument.

**What do they want?**

They want their piano to sound beautiful and play like it should. They want to sit down, press a key, and feel that everything is right — the tone is clear, the action is smooth, and the experience is effortless. For many, there's a deep sentimental connection to the instrument, and having it properly tuned and maintained brings a sense of pride and clarity, like driving a brand new car off the lot. A great-sounding piano doesn't just serve the player — it fills the room and moves everyone who listens.

---

## **2. Has a Problem**

**External Problem (What is happening to them?)**

- Their piano is slowly degrading — it doesn't sound like it used to.
- Strings and tuning pins are loosening over time.
- The key action is getting wobbly and uneven.
- The whole instrument feels misaligned and off, producing a noticeably worse sound.

**Internal Problem (How does this make them feel?)**

- Playing is less enjoyable and feels like a chore.
- Playing for others becomes embarrassing — they know it sounds off.
- The disconnect between what they hear and what they expect is frustrating and annoying.
- They lose motivation to sit down and practice.
- Over time, their skills decline because they aren't playing as much.

**Philosophical Problem (Why is this unfair/wrong?)**

- A musician who cares about quality sound shouldn't be held back by a neglected instrument.
- The whole point of owning a piano is the experience of playing and listening — a poorly maintained piano robs them of that.
- It prevents them from being able to entertain family and friends with confidence.
- It diminishes the luster of a skill they've worked hard to cultivate.
- Uneven keys and poor action can actually train bad technique, undermining years of practice.
